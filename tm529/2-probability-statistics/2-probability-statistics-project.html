[30] Late one night in a bar in Las Vegas you meet a guy who claims to know that in the casino at the Tropicana there are two sorts of slot machines: one that pays out 3% of the time, and one that pays out 25% of the time. You find a red and a blue machine side by side. You toss a coin to decide which machine to try first; based on this you then put the coin into the red machine. It does not pay out. How should you update your estimate of the probability that this is the high payout machine? What if it had paid out - what would be your new estimate then? Consider how many times you would have to fail to get a payout from the red machine before being say 90% confident it is not the high payout machine.
